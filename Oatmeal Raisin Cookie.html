OATMEAL RAISIN COOKIES

Our signature oatmeal raisin cookies are a masterclass in texture—crisped to perfection at the edges with a dense, decadently chewy heart that melts at the first touch. Rich, comforting, and unapologetically indulgent—they’re not just a sweet treat; they’re an exquisite moment of pure, nostalgic bliss in every single bite.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup Unsalted Butter (Softened to room temperature)
  • 1 cup Light Brown Sugar (Packed firmly for a deep molasses chew)
  • 0.25 cup Granulated White Sugar
  • 2 Large Eggs (Room temperature)
  • 1 tablespoon Pure Vanilla Extract
  • 1.5 cups All-Purpose Flour (Sifted)
  • 1 teaspoon Baking Soda
  • 1.5 teaspoons Ground Cinnamon (For that signature warm spice depth)
  • 0.5 teaspoon Salt
  • 3 cups Old-Fashioned Rolled Oats (Do not use instant oats)
  • 1.5 cups Juicy Seedless Raisins

    • DIRECTIONS

    1. Cream the Base: In a large bowl or stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment, beat the softened butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar together on medium-high speed for about 2 minutes until completely smooth and creamy.
    2. Add Wet Emulsions: Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Incorporate the pure vanilla extract and beat on high speed for 1 minute until the mixture is light and uniform.
    3. Combine Dry Spices: In a separate medium bowl, whisk together the sifted flour, baking soda, ground cinnamon, and salt. Turn the mixer to low speed and slowly fold the dry ingredients into the wet cream until just combined.
    4. Fold Oats & Raisins: Add the old-fashioned rolled oats and juicy raisins to the bowl. Use a heavy silicone spatula to fold them into the thick dough by hand, ensuring the fruit is evenly distributed.
    5. The Essential Chill: Cover the cookie dough tightly with plastic wrap and chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 to 45 minutes. Chilling prevents the cookies from spreading too thin, ensuring a thick, chewy center.
    6. Portion and Bake: Preheat your oven to 350°F (177°C). Line two large baking sheets with parchment paper. Scoop large balls of dough (about 2 tablespoons each) and place them 2 inches apart on the sheets. Bake for 11 to 13 minutes until the outer edges are lightly golden but the centers still look soft.
    7. Cool to Set: Remove from the oven and let the cookies rest on the hot baking sheet for 5 minutes to set their structure. Transfer them carefully to a wire rack to cool down completely.
