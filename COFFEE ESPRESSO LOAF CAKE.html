COFFEE ESPRESSO LOAF CAKE

A rich, robust, and comforting treat designed for Father's Day. This loaf cake features a deeply aromatic crumb infused with premium espresso shots and high-quality dark cocoa, generously studded with pocketed chocolate chips and finished with a rustic crust of chopped, toasted walnuts.

INGREDIENTS

  • 200g all-purpose flour & 40g Dutch-process cocoa powder
  • 2 tablespoons instant espresso powder (dissolved in 60ml warm water)
  • 150g soft brown sugar (for extra moisture and molasses notes)
  • 100g unsalted butter (melted) & 2 large eggs
  • 100ml sour cream or thick Greek yogurt (to keep the crumb tender)
  • 75g dark chocolate chips (folded into the batter)
  • 50g walnuts, coarsely chopped (for the topping crust)

DIRECTIONS

  1. Whisk together the all-purpose flour, cocoa powder, sugar, baking powder, and a pinch of salt in a large bowl.
  2. In a separate bowl, blend the melted butter, eggs, sour cream, and the concentrated warm espresso liquid until uniform.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry mixture, folding gently with a spatula just until combined to maintain a light structure.
  4. Gently fold the dark chocolate chips evenly throughout the thick batter.
  5. Spoon the batter into a greased and parchment-lined loaf pan, smoothing the surface with the back of your spoon.
  6. Shower the top of the batter generously with a dense layer of the coarsely chopped toasted walnuts.
  7. Bake at 175°C (345°F) for 45-50 minutes, or until a skewer inserted into the center comes out clean. Let cool before slicing into rustic thick sheets.
