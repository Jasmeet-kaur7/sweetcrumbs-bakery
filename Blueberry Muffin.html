BLUEBERRY MUFFIN

Twice the size of normal muffins, these bakery-style jumbo treats are soft, moist, and bursting with real fruit. Infused with a bright hint of fresh lemon, they feature a gooey jammy blueberry top layer and are piled high with a crispy, buttery cinnamon crumble.

INGREDIENTS

    For the Crunchy Crumble Layer:

    • 0.5 cup All-Purpose Flour
    • 3 tablespoons Brown Sugar
    • 2.5 tablespoons Vegan Butter (Cold or melted)
    • 0.25 teaspoon Ground Cinnamon

    For the Fluffy Muffin Batter:

    • 2.5 cups All-Purpose Flour
    • 1 tablespoon Baking Powder
    • 0.5 teaspoon Salt
    • 0.75 cup Cane Sugar or Coconut Sugar
    • 1 cup Plant-Based Milk (Oat, soy, or almond milk)
    • 0.33 cup Neutral Oil (Or melted vegan butter)
    • 1 tablespoon Fresh Lemon Juice & Zest (Brightens the berry flavors)
    • 1.5 cups Fresh Blueberries (Plus extra for the top layer)

DIRECTIONS

  1. Preheat & Prepare: Preheat your oven to 400°F (204°C). Line a jumbo muffin pan with 6 paper liners, or space regular liners out in a standard tin to give the tops room to expand.
  2. Make the Crumble: In a small bowl, mix the flour, brown sugar, and cinnamon. Rub in the vegan butter with your fingertips until it forms chunky, pea-sized crumbs. Set aside.
  3. Whisk Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Toss the blueberries with 1 tablespoon of this flour mixture to prevent them from sinking to the bottom.
  4. Mix Wet & Combine: In a separate bowl, whisk together the plant milk, oil, lemon juice, and lemon zest. Pour into the dry ingredients and fold gently with a spatula until just mixed. Do not overmix, or the crumb will become heavy.
  5. Assemble and Bake: Divide the batter evenly among your lined muffin cups. Press a few extra blueberries onto the top of each muffin to create a jammy look, then pile the sweet crumble mixture generously over the top.
  6. The Temperature Trick: Bake at 400°F (204°C) for the first 5 minutes to force a rapid rise, then reduce the heat to 375°F (190°C) and bake for another 20-25 minutes until a toothpick inserted comes out clean. Cool on a wire rack.
