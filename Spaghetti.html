SPAHETTI

This Spaghetti Carbonara is a Roman culinary masterpiece celebrating rich, velvet textures and savory depths. Long strands of al dente spaghetti are glossy and beautifully coated in a creamy emulsified sauce made purely from fresh eggs and sharp grated cheese. Crispy, golden pieces of pancetta or bacon are tossed throughout, finished with an abundant dusting of coarse black pepper and chopped fresh parsley.

INGREDIENTS

  • Pasta: 200g high-quality spaghetti
  • Meat: 100g pancetta, guanciale, or thick-cut bacon (diced)
  • Eggs: 2 large whole eggs and 1 large egg yolk (at room temperature)
  • Cheese: ½ cup freshly grated Pecorino Romano or Parmesan cheese
  • Herbs: 2 tbsp fresh Italian parsley, finely chopped
  • Seasoning: 1 tsp coarsely cracked black pepper and salt for the pasta water

DIRECTIONS

  1. Boil the Pasta: Cook spaghetti in a large pot of boiling salted water until it is just al dente. Reserve ½ cup of starchy pasta water before draining.
  2. Crisp the Meat: While the pasta cooks, heat a large skillet over medium heat. Sauté the diced pancetta or bacon for 5 to 6 minutes until crisp and golden brown. Turn off the heat.
  3. Whisk the Egg Mixture: In a separate bowl, vigorously whisk together the whole eggs, egg yolk, grated cheese, and plenty of cracked black pepper until a thick paste forms.
  4. Emulsify the Sauce: Add the hot, drained spaghetti directly into the skillet with the crisped meat and fat. Pour in the egg and cheese mixture along with 2-3 tablespoons of reserved warm pasta water. Toss rapidly off the heat so the residual heat cooks the eggs into a smooth, creamy sauce without scrambling them.
  5. Garnish and Serve: Keep tossing until the sauce thickens and tightly clings to the pasta strands. Top with additional grated cheese, cracked black pepper, and fresh parsley. Serve immediately.
