PLAIN SALTINE CRACKERS

Feathery light, beautifully docked, and shatteringly crisp, our savory crackers are layered with precision to deliver a clean, toasted finish. Subtle, sophisticated, and flawlessly textured—they are engineered not just to carry your finest cheeses and spreads, but to accentuate them with a sharp, elegant crunch.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1.5 cups All-Purpose Flour (Plus extra for dusting your rolling surface)
  • 0.5 teaspoon Fine Sea Salt (For the dough mixture)
  • 0.5 teaspoon Baking Powder (Crucial for creating signature air bubbles)
  • 2 tablespoons Cold Unsalted Butter (Cubed into tiny pieces)
  • 2 tablespoons Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • 0.5 cup Cold Water (Adjust as needed to form a cohesive dough)
  • Coarse Flaky Sea Salt (For dusting heavily on top)

DIRECTIONS

  1. Combine Dry Bases: In a large mixing bowl, thoroughly whisk together the all-purpose flour, fine sea salt, and baking powder to distribute the leavening agents evenly.
  2. Cut in the Fats: Add the tiny cubes of cold unsalted butter and extra virgin olive oil into the dry bowl. Rub the fats into the flour using your fingertips or a pastry cutter until the entire mixture looks like coarse sand.
  3. Form a Firm Dough: Gradually stream in the cold water a few tablespoons at a time, tossing with a fork until a rough, cohesive dough begins to bundle. Transfer to a lightly floured board and knead for 2 minutes until uniform. Wrap in cling wrap and rest for 15 minutes to relax the gluten.
  4. Roll Out Paper-Thin: Divide the dough into two sections. Roll the dough directly onto a sheet of parchment paper as thin as physically possible—ideally until it is nearly transparent (less than 1/16 inch thick). If the dough fights back, rest it for 5 minutes and continue rolling.
  5. Cut and Dock: Use a rolling pizza cutter or a fluted pastry wheel to score the dough sheet into 2-inch uniform squares (do not separate them completely). Use the tines of a fork to prick each square 4–5 times. This docking step is critical to release internal steam so they don't inflate like pillows.
  6. Bake Until Crispy: Lightly spritz the tops with water and scatter a heavy layer of flaky sea salt over the squares. Transfer the parchment sheet onto a hot baking tray. Bake at 400°F (204°C) for 10–12 minutes, watching very closely. They are finished when the edges are deep golden-brown.
  7. Cool to Snap: Immediately move the crackers off the hot tray and onto a wire cooling rack. Allow them to cool completely to room temperature; this step locks in their crisp, snappy texture. Store in an airtight glass container.
