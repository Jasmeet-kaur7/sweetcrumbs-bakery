ESPRESSO

Espresso is a concentrated coffee beverage brewed by forcing hot water under high pressure through finely-ground coffee beans. Originating in Italy, it is characterized by its bold flavor, thick body, and a signature layer of reddish-brown foam on top known as the crema.

INGREDIENTS

  • Finely ground coffee beans (18–20 grams for a double shot)
  • Filtered water (approx. 60 ml)

DIRECTIONS

  1. Grind fresh coffee beans to a fine consistency resembling table salt.
  2. Transfer the grounds into a clean, dry portafilter basket.
  3. Use a tamper to press the grounds down evenly with firm, level pressure.
  4. Lock the portafilter into the espresso machine group head.
  5. Place a pre-warmed cup beneath the spouts.
  6. Start the brew cycle immediately, extracting for 25 to 30 seconds until the stream turns light and blonde.
