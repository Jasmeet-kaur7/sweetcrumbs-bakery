NEAPOLITAN PIZZA

This Skillet Neapolitan Pizza brings traditional artisan baking techniques right to your home stovetop. Using a hot cast-iron skillet creates a beautifully raised, puffy crust mimicking wood-fired textures. A bright tomato base is layered with pockets of fresh melted mozzarella cheese, smoky pepperoni slices, and rich kalamata olives. Crisped to perfection with a golden rim, it delivers an incredible gourmet profile.

INGREDIENTS

  • Pizza Dough: 1 fresh high-hydration artisan dough ball
  • Sauce: ⅓ cup crushed San Marzano tomatoes
  • Cheese: ½ cup fresh mozzarella cheese, torn into chunks
  • Toppings: 10-12 thin slices of pepperoni and ¼ cup pitted kalamata olives
  • Fat: 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil (for drizzling and greasing)
  • Seasoning: A pinch of sea salt and dried oregano

DIRECTIONS

  1. Preheat the Broiler: Set your home oven to broiler mode on high heat. Place your empty cast-iron skillet on the stovetop over high heat for 5 minutes until completely hot.
  2. Shape the Crust: Stretch your fresh pizza dough out on a floured surface to form a circle matching your skillet diameter, leaving the outer edge slightly puffy.
  3. Sear the Bottom: Drizzle a tiny drop of olive oil into the hot skillet. Carefully lay the stretched dough inside. It will bubble up immediately. Cook for 1 to 2 minutes on the stove to crisp the bottom.
  4. Add Toppings Quickly: While the dough is still searing on the stovetop, spread the crushed tomatoes over the base. Drop the fresh mozzarella chunks, pepperoni slices, and kalamata olives evenly across the sauce layer.
  5. Broil to Finish: Transfer the skillet immediately to the top rack under the oven broiler. Cook for 3 to 4 minutes until the outer crust turns deep golden-brown with char spots and the cheese bubbles beautifully. Drizzle with extra virgin olive oil before slicing.
