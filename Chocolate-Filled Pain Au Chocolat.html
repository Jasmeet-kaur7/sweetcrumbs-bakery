CHOCOLATE FILLED PAIN AU CHOCOLATE

Transport your kitchen straight to a Parisian patisserie. These traditional French pastries feature golden, hyper-flaky laminated puff layers wrapping around two classic semi-sweet dark chocolate baking batons.

INGREDIENTS

  • 480g Wheat Flour (Chilled or cold for best stability)
  • 75g Granulated Sugar
  • 30g Fresh Yeast (Or 10g Active Dry Yeast)
  • 10g Fine Salt
  • 50g Egg (Approx. 1 large egg, whisked)
  • 110g Whole Milk (Cold)
  • 86g Water (Cold)
  • 250g High-Quality Unsalted Butter (Chilled, flat sheet for lamination)
  • 24 Dark Chocolate Batons / Sticks (44% to 50% cocoa content)

DIRECTIONS

  1. Prepare the Butter Block: Place your cold butter between sheets of parchment paper. Pound with a rolling pin into a flat rectangle exactly half the size of your rolled-out dough sheet. Keep cool.
  2. Lock in the Butter: Roll your chilled dough out. Place the cold butter sheet in the center and fold the sides of the dough over it, sealing it like an envelope so no butter peeks out.
  3. The Lamination Folds: Roll the dough sheet out lengthwise. Complete a letter-fold (tri-fold), turn 90 degrees, and roll out again to repeat the fold. Wrap tightly and rest in the fridge for 30 minutes. Repeat this process to create dozens of microscopic flaky layers.
  4. Shape the Pastries: Roll the final rested dough out into a long rectangle roughly 4mm thick. Slice into 12 even rectangles. Place one chocolate baton on the edge, roll over once, place a second baton, and finish rolling into a tidy log.
  5. Proofing: Place the pastries seam-side down on a parchment-lined baking sheet. Let them rise at a cool room temperature (under 72°F / 22°C) for 1.5 to 2 hours until puffed and jiggly.
  6. Bake to Perfection: Brush gently with an egg wash mixture. Bake at 200°C (392°F) in a conventional oven for 13-15 minutes until heavily deep-golden, crisp, and hollow-sounding when tapped.
