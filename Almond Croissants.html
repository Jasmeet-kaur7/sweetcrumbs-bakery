ALMOND CROISSANT

Give day-old croissants a luxurious upgrade with this classic French bakery method. Soaked in a light sugar syrup, filled with a rich and aromatic almond frangipane paste, and baked until crispy, these croissants are finished with a mountain of sliced almonds and a heavy dust of powdered sugar.

INGREDIENTS

    For the Simple Soaking Syrup:

    • 0.5 cup Water
    • 0.33 cup Granulated Sugar
    • 1 teaspoon Amaretto Liquer or Rum (Optional, for deep aroma)

    For the Almond Cream Filling (Frangipane):

    • 100g Unsalted Butter (Room temperature, softened)
    • 100g Granulated Sugar
    • 100g Almond Flour (Finely ground)
    • 1 tablespoon All-Purpose Flour
    • 1 Large Egg (Room temperature)
    • 1 teaspoon Pure Vanilla Extract
    • 0.5 teaspoon Almond Extract

    For Assembly & Topping:

    • 6 Large Croissants (Preferably 1 or 2 days old)
    • 0.5 cup Sliced Blanched Almonds
    • Powdered Sugar (For a heavy dusting at the end)

DIRECTIONS

  1. Preheat & Prep: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a large baking tray with parchment paper.
  2. Boil the Syrup: In a small saucepan over medium heat, combine the water and sugar. Bring to a gentle boil for 1-2 minutes until the sugar fully dissolves. Stir in the vanilla or amaretto, then remove from heat to cool down slightly.
  3. Whip up the Frangipane: In a medium mixing bowl, cream the softened butter and granulated sugar together until smooth. Add the almond flour, all-purpose flour, egg, vanilla extract, and almond extract. Beat well with an electric mixer or whisk until the almond paste is fluffy and uniform.
  4. Slice and Soak: Use a serrated knife to slice the day-old croissants in half horizontally, leaving them slightly attached at the back hinge like a book. Use a pastry brush to generously soak both inside cut faces and the outer top crust with the warm sugar syrup.
  5. Fill and Close: Spread a thick, generous layer of the almond cream (about 2-3 tablespoons) inside each croissant. Close the croissants, then spread a thin layer of remaining almond cream over the outer top surface. (The top layer acts as the glue for your toppings!)
  6. Garnish and Bake: Press the top of each croissant firmly into the bowl of sliced almonds so they stick heavily to the paste. Arrange them onto the baking sheet. Bake for 15-18 minutes until the almond cream inside sets and the sliced toppings are beautifully toasted and golden brown.
  7. The Finishing Touch: Remove the tray from the oven and let them settle for 5 minutes. Transfer to a cooling rack and dust heavily with powdered sugar right before serving alongside a hot cup of coffee.
