ARTISANAL SUMMER BERRY GALETTE

Our Artisanal Summer Berry Galette is a celebration of rustic European fruit baking. Hand-folded with a rich, flaky all-butter pastry crust, this open-faced tart holds a vibrant, jammy center overflowing with fresh local summer berries. Featuring red currants, gooseberries, and wild mountain raspberries, it is baked on parchment to catch every sweet drop of caramelized fruit nectar—offering an absolute masterclass in seasonal elegance.

INGREDIENTS

  • Flaky Pâte Brisée Pastry: 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour, ½ cup ice-cold unsalted butter (cubed), 1 tbsp sugar, pinch of salt, ice water
  • The Berry Medley Core: 2 cups mixed fresh berries (red currants, gooseberries, raspberries, and white currants)
  • Thickener & Binder: 2 tbsp cornstarch, ¼ cup cane sugar (adjusted for berry tartness)
  • Glaze Finish: 1 large egg (beaten for egg wash), 1 tbsp coarse turbinado sugar for the crust crust edge
  • Aromatic Lift: 1 tsp fresh lemon zest, ½ tsp pure vanilla extract

DIRECTIONS

  1. Hand-Craft the Pastry Dough: Toss cubed ice-cold butter into the flour, sugar, and salt mixture. Work it with your fingertips until it resembles coarse crumbs. Drizzle in ice water bit by bit until the dough just holds together. Shape into a smooth flat disc, wrap in wrap film, and chill for 1 hour.
  2. Prep Your Oven: Preheat your oven to 400°F (205°C) and line a large baking sheet securely with non-stick baking parchment paper.
  3. Toss the Fruit Filling: In a medium bowl, gently combine the fresh red currants, gooseberries, and raspberries with the cane sugar, cornstarch, lemon zest, and vanilla extract until evenly glossy.
  4. Roll & Assemble Rustically: On a floured surface, roll the chilled dough disc out into an imperfect 12-inch circle. Transfer carefully onto the parchment sheet. Mound your fruit filling directly into the center, leaving a clean 2-inch border around all sides.
  5. Fold and Bake: Gently pleat and fold the edges of the pastry dough up and over the fruit filling, leaving the center open. Brush the folded dough edges with egg wash and scatter coarse turbinado sugar flakes on top. Bake for 35 to 40 minutes until the pastry is deeply golden-brown and the fruit juices bubble intensely. Cool slightly before slicing.
