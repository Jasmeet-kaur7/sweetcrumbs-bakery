CHOCOLATE HAZELNUT COOKIES

Our signature chocolate hazelnut cookies are a masterclass in textures—generously crowned with a dense, crackling mosaic of roasted hazelnuts that give way to a rich, deeply fudgy chocolate center. Intense, rustic, and unapologetically decadent—they are not just a simple treat; they are an exquisite interplay of crunch and velvet crafted to elevate your sweet indulgence.

INGREDIENTS

  • 175g Unsalted Butter (Softened at room temperature)
  • 150g Granulated White Sugar
  • 100g Light Brown Sugar (Provides a rich, toffee-like chewiness)
  • 2 Large Free-Range Eggs (Lightly beaten)
  • 425g Plain All-Purpose Flour
  • 2/3 cup Unsweetened Cocoa Powder (High-quality Dutch-processed for maximum depth)
  • 0.5 teaspoon Baking Powder
  • 0.5 teaspoon Baking Soda / Bread Soda
  • 1.5 teaspoons Sea Salt (Divided: 1 tsp for dough, 0.5 tsp for dusting)
  • 100g Whole Blanched Hazelnuts (Roasted and roughly chopped)
  • 200g Dark Chocolate Shards (Roughly chopped into chunky pieces)

DIRECTIONS

  1. Cream the Base: In the bowl of a free-standing mixer fitted with a paddle attachment, beat the softened butter, white sugar, and brown sugar on a high speed until the mixture becomes light, pale, and ultra-fluffy (approx. 5 minutes).
  2. Sift Dry Ingredients: In a medium-sized bowl, thoroughly whisk together the plain flour, cocoa powder, 1 teaspoon of sea salt, baking powder, and baking soda to remove any structural lumps.
  3. Combine and Fold: Turn the mixer speed to medium-low. Slowly incorporate the lightly beaten eggs. Gradually add the sifted dry mixture, mixing until just combined. Fold in the dark chocolate chunks and chopped roasted hazelnuts with a silicone spatula. Do not overmix, or the crumb will become rubbery.
  4. Portion and Freeze: Divide the heavy dough into 10-12 large, equal-sized portions. Roll each portion firmly into uniform spheres. Arrange them onto a tray lined with parchment paper, press down slightly, and place in the freezer for at least 4 hours (or overnight) until completely solid.
  5. Preheat and Arrange: When ready to bake, preheat your conventional oven to 180°C (350°F / Gas Mark 4). Line a heavy-duty baking sheet with fresh baking paper and space the frozen cookie balls at least 5cm apart to allow room for spreading.
  6. Bake to Perfection: Finely scatter the remaining 0.5 teaspoon of coarse sea salt over the cookie tops. Bake in the preheated oven for exactly 15 minutes until the outer shell expands into a cracked, crisp mosaic but the centers remain soft to the touch.
  7. Cool Down: Let the cookies rest on the hot baking tray for 5 minutes to set their structure, then transfer gently with a flat spatula to a wire cooling grid. Serve warm alongside a cold glass of milk or dark espresso.
