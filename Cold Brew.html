COLD BREW

A crisp, naturally sweet, and highly caffeinated cold coffee made by steeping coarsely ground coffee beans in room temperature or chilled water for an extended period. Because it is never exposed to heat, it features exceptionally low acidity and a smooth, chocolatey flavor profile.

INGREDIENTS

  • 100 grams coarsely ground coffee beans (resembling coarse sea salt)
  • 400 ml filtered water (room temperature or cold)
  • Ice cubes (for serving)
  • Milk, cream, or simple syrup (optional, to taste)

DIRECTIONS

  1. Add the coarsely ground coffee beans to a large glass jar or a cold brew pitcher.
  2. Pour the filtered water over the coffee grounds and stir gently to ensure all grounds are thoroughly wet.
  3. Seal the container and let it steep at room temperature or in the refrigerator for 12 to 18 hours.
  4. Strain the coffee mixture through a fine-mesh sieve lined with a paper coffee filter or cheesecloth to remove all sediment.
  5. Fill a clean serving glass generously with ice cubes.
  6. Pour the smooth cold brew concentrate over the ice, diluting with a splash of extra water or milk if preferred.
