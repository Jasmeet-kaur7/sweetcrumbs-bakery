PINK SAUCE PASTA

This Creamy Pink Sauce Rigatoni (also known as Parma Rosa sauce) offers the absolute best of both worlds by seamlessly blending a vibrant tomato marinara with a rich, velvety cream sauce. Large ridges on the rigatoni pasta perfectly hold the smooth, blushing pink sauce, which is infused with garlic, Italian herbs, and lots of freshly grated Parmesan cheese. Garnishments of aromatic fresh basil leaves and fiery red chilli flakes make this a restaurant-worthy staple.

INGREDIENTS

  • Pasta: 200g rigatoni pasta
  • Tomato Base: 1 cup tomato puree or canned crushed marinara tomatoes
  • Cream Base: ½ cup heavy cream or cooking cream
  • Aromatics: 4 cloves garlic (finely minced) & 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • Cheese: ⅓ cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • Herbs & Spices: 1 tsp dried oregano, 1 tsp red chilli flakes, and a handful of fresh basil leaves
  • Fat: 1.5 tbsp extra virgin olive oil or butter
  • Seasoning: Salt and freshly cracked black pepper to taste

DIRECTIONS

  1. Boil the Rigatoni: Drop the rigatoni into a deep pot of heavily salted boiling water. Cook until just al dente according to the package instructions. Reserve ¼ cup of pasta water, then drain the noodles and set aside.
  2. Sauté the Aromatics: Heat olive oil or melt butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Sauté the chopped onions and minced garlic for 3 minutes until soft, sweet, and fragrant.
  3. Simmer the Red Base: Pour in the tomato puree, dried oregano, half of the chilli flakes, salt, and black pepper. Stir thoroughly and lower the heat to medium-low. Let it simmer for 6 to 8 minutes until the raw tomato aroma disappears.
  4. Blend into Pink Sauce: Turn the heat to low. Slowly pour in the heavy cream while stirring continuously to beautifully transform the red sauce into a rich, silky pink color. Stir in most of the grated Parmesan cheese until completely melted.
  5. Toss and Finish: Add the cooked rigatoni and torn fresh basil leaves into the pink sauce. Toss gently for 1 to 2 minutes until the sauce heavily coats the ridges of the pasta, adding a splash of reserved pasta water if too thick. Plate immediately and garnish with remaining Parmesan, chilli flakes, and fresh basil sprigs.
