PANEER JUNGLEE SANDWICH

The Paneer Junglee Sandwich is a popular, refreshing cafe-style classic known for its creamy, cold stuffing and crunchy textures. Unlike hot toasted variants, this sandwich features a rich filling of finely crumbled or shredded paneer (cottage cheese) bound together with a spiced mayonnaise and green herb dressing. Mixed with finely chopped capsicum, carrots, and black pepper, it is loaded into soft whole-grain or multigrain bread, creating a satisfying, quick-prep snack perfect for high-tea or lunch boxes.

INGREDIENTS

  • Bread: 4 slices of multigrain, whole-grain, or brown bread
  • Paneer: 1 cup paneer, finely crumbled or grated
  • Creamy Dressing: 3-4 tbsp mayonnaise (or hung curd)
  • Vegetables: ¼ cup finely chopped capsicum, ¼ cup grated carrot, and 1 tbsp finely cut coriander
  • Seasoning: ½ tsp freshly crushed black pepper, ½ tsp roasted cumin powder, and salt to taste
  • Spicy Add-ins: 1 finely chopped green chilli (optional) or a dash of red chilli flakes
  • Accompaniment: Tomato ketchup or sweet chilli sauce for dipping

DIRECTIONS

  1. Prepare the Junglee Dressing: In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, black pepper, roasted cumin powder, and salt until completely smooth.
  2. Fold in the Fillings: Add the finely crumbled paneer, chopped capsicum, grated carrots, green chillies, and fresh coriander into the bowl. Mix gently until the paneer and veggies are evenly coated in the creamy dressing.
  3. Assemble the Base: Place your multigrain bread slices on a flat surface. Trim the edges if you prefer a traditional soft, clean cafe-style presentation.
  4. Stuff the Sandwich: Spread a generous, thick layer of the cold paneer mixture across two of the bread slices, smoothing it all the way out to the edges.
  5. Slice and Serve: Top with the remaining bread slices, press down gently to seal the filling, and slice cleanly diagonally into large triangles. Serve cold or at room temperature paired with a side bowl of tomato ketchup.
