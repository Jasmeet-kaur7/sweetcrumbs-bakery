CHOCO CHIP MUFFIN

Get that authentic bakery feel right at home with this simple recipe! These golden-topped chocolate chip muffins are incredibly soft, moist, and packed with sweet mini or semi-sweet chocolate morsels in every single bite.

INGREDIENTS

  • 2.5 cups All-Purpose Flour
  • 1 tablespoon Baking Powder
  • 0.5 teaspoon Baking Soda
  • 0.5 teaspoon Salt
  • 0.5 cup Unsalted Butter (Melted)
  • 0.75 cup Granulated White Sugar
  • 2 Large Eggs
  • 1 cup Buttermilk (Or whole milk)
  • 1.5 cups Semi-Sweet Chocolate Chips (Divided)
  • 2 teaspoons Vanilla Extract

DIRECTIONS

  1. Prep Your Station: Preheat your oven to 425°F (218°C). Line a standard 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners or spray with non-stick cooking spray.
  2. Whisk Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Toss 1.25 cups of chocolate chips into the dry mix (this prevents them from sinking to the bottom).
  3. Combine Wet Ingredients: In a medium bowl, thoroughly whisk the melted butter, sugar, eggs, milk/buttermilk, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  4. Merge the Batter: Pour the wet ingredients into the dry bowl. Stir together gently using a spatula until JUST combined. Leave it slightly lumpy; overmixing makes muffins dense.
  5. Fill and Bake: Divide the batter evenly into your muffin tray. Press the remaining 0.25 cup of chocolate chips into the tops of each muffin. Bake at 425°F (218°C) for 7 minutes, then lower temperature to 350°F (177°C) and bake for an additional 13-15 minutes.
  6. Cool Down: Let the muffins rest in the hot pan for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool down completely.
