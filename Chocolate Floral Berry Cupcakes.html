CHOCOLATE FLORAL BERRY CUPCAKES

Our signature Mother's Day Berry Rosette Cupcakes are a masterclass in elegant confectionery—featuring an incredibly moist, dark cocoa sponge base piled high with an ultra-smooth vanilla bean buttercream swirl. Each piece is meticulously decorated with handcrafted sugar paste rosettes, delicate pastel blossoms, and clustering deep-berry sugar pearls. Refined, exquisite, and unapologetically beautiful—they offer an enchanting floral experience in every luxurious bite.

INGREDIENTS

    For the Velvet Cocoa Cupcakes (Yields 12):

    • 150g All-Purpose Flour (Sifted)
    • 35g Dutch-Process Cocoa Powder
    • 150g Caster Sugar
    • 0.75 teaspoon Baking Soda
    • 0.5 teaspoon Fine Sea Salt
    • 1 Large Egg (Room temperature)
    • 120ml Buttermilk (Provides a tender, melt-in-your-mouth crumb)
    • 60ml Vegetable Oil
    • 1 teaspoon Pure Vanilla Extract
    • 100ml Warm Water

    For the Buttercream & Floral Garnishes:

    • 200g Unsalted Butter (Softened at room temperature)
    • 400g Powdered Icing Sugar (Sifted)
    • 2-3 tablespoons Heavy Cream
    • 1 teaspoon Vanilla Bean Paste (For beautiful real speckles)
    • Pink & Burgundy Fondant/Sugar Paste (For sculpting the rosettes and blossoms)
    • Glossy Dark Berry Sugar Pearls / Nonpareils
    • Edible Silver Dragées (For the rosette centers)

DIRECTIONS

  1. Bake the Bases: Preheat your oven to 175°C (350°F) and line a 12-hole cupcake tin with deep chocolate-colored paper liners. Whisk the dry ingredients (flour, cocoa, sugar, baking soda, salt) in a large bowl. Add the egg, buttermilk, oil, and vanilla extract, whisking until smooth. Gradually stir in the warm water. Divide the smooth batter evenly and bake for 16-18 minutes until springy. Cool completely.
  2. Whip the Buttercream: In a stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment, beat the softened butter for 5 minutes until pale and creamy. Gradually add the sifted icing sugar in batches, alternating with the heavy cream. Stir in the vanilla bean paste and beat on high speed for 3-4 minutes until light, fluffy, and perfectly pipeable.
  3. Sculpt the Sugar Flowers: Roll out your pink fondant thinly. Use a small round plunger cutter to create petals, wrapping them around each other tightly to form a blooming rosette core. Use burgundy fondant to create tiny blossoms and cluster small edible sugar pearls together to mimic wild blackberries. Drop a single silver dragée into each rose center. Set aside to dry slightly.
  4. Pipe the Swirls: Fit a piping bag with a large open round or wide star nozzle and fill it with the white vanilla bean buttercream. Pipe a clean, uniform, and stable flat-top dome swirl onto each cooled cupcake.
  5. Assemble the Bouquet Display: Carefully arrange one large pink sugar rosette slightly off-center on top of the buttercream base. Cluster your handcrafted burgundy berry pearls and mini blossoms tightly around the rose to secure them into the frosting, creating a stunning, compact floral bouquet look.
