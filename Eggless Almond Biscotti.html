EGGLESS ALMOND BISCOTTI

Our signature almond cantuccini are a masterclass in classic Italian baking—twice-baked to achieve a definitive, shattering snap that yields a beautiful golden crumb studded with whole roasted almonds. Crisp, timeless, and effortlessly elegant—they are not just a simple biscuit; they are a refined sensory ritual designed to be slowly savored alongside your finest espresso or vin santo.

INGREDIENTS

  • 250g Whole Blanched Almonds (Unsalted, lightly toasted)
  • 350g Plain All-Purpose Flour (Sifted)
  • 250g Granulated White Sugar
  • 1 teaspoon Baking Powder
  • 0.25 teaspoon Fine Sea Salt
  • 3 Large Eggs (Room temperature)
  • 1 teaspoon Pure Vanilla Extract
  • 0.5 teaspoon Almond Extract (For a deeply aromatic profile)
  • Zest of 1 Fresh Orange (Finely grated for a bright citrus note)
  • 1 Egg White (Beaten, for the shiny top crust glaze)

DIRECTIONS

  1. Toast the Nuts: Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F). Spread the whole almonds evenly onto a baking sheet and toast for 8-10 minutes until aromatic and pale golden inside. Remove and allow to cool completely.
  2. Combine Dry Bases: In a large mixing bowl, thoroughly whisk together the sifted all-purpose flour, white sugar, baking powder, and fine sea salt until uniform.
  3. Whisk and Form Batter: In a separate bowl, whisk together the 3 whole eggs, vanilla extract, almond extract, and finely grated orange zest. Pour the wet mixture into the dry ingredients. Stir with a wooden spoon until a sticky, firm dough begins to coalesce, then fold in the whole toasted almonds.
  4. Shape the Logs: Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured work surface. Divide it into two equal portions. Using lightly floured hands, shape each portion into a long, flat log roughly 30cm long and 5cm wide. Transfer both logs onto a baking sheet lined with fresh parchment paper, leaving ample space between them.
  5. First Bake: Brush the tops of the logs generously with the beaten egg white for a classic shiny finish. Bake in the preheated oven at 180°C (350°F) for 20-25 minutes until the logs are firm to the touch and lightly golden. Remove from the oven and let cool on the tray for exactly 10 minutes. Lower the oven temperature to 150°C (300°F).
  6. Slice on the Bias: Transfer the warm logs to a cutting board. Using a sharp serrated bread knife, slice the logs diagonally at a 45-degree angle into slices roughly 1.5cm thick to reveal the cross-sections of the embedded whole almonds.
  7. The Second Crisp Bake: Arrange the sliced cantuccini cut-side down back onto the parchment-lined baking sheet. Return to the oven at 150°C (300°F) and bake for an additional 10-12 minutes, turning them over halfway through, until they are completely dry, crisp, and golden-brown. Cool completely on a wire rack to lock in their signature snap.
