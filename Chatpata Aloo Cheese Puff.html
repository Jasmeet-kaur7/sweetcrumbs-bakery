CHATPATA ALOO CHEESE PUFF

The Chatpata Aloo Cheese Puff is a bakery-style savory pastry that combines a flaky, multi-layered golden crust with a rich, spiced filling. The center features a tangy, flavorful mashed potato mixture (aloo masala) layered with melted gooey cheese. Seasoned with traditional street-style spices and baked to golden-brown perfection, it makes an excellent teatime snack when paired with spicy green chutney and sweet tomato ketchup.

INGREDIENTS

  • Pastry: 1 pack pre-made puff pastry sheets (thawed)
  • Potatoes: 3 medium potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed
  • Cheese: ½ cup mozzarella or processed cheddar, grated
  • Aromatics: 1 small onion (finely chopped), 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste, and 1 green chilli (finely chopped)
  • Chatpata Spices: ½ tsp turmeric powder, ½ tsp red chilli powder, ½ tsp garam masala, 1 tsp chaat masala, and ½ tsp amchur (dry mango) powder
  • Herbs & Seeds: ½ tsp cumin seeds, 2 tbsp fresh coriander leaves (finely chopped)
  • Finishers: 2 tbsp milk or melted butter (for the top wash), and 1 tbsp oil

DIRECTIONS

  1. Prepare the Filling: Heat 1 tbsp oil in a pan. Splutter the cumin seeds, then sauté onions, green chilli, and ginger-garlic paste until translucent. Add the mashed potatoes, turmeric, red chilli powder, garam masala, chaat masala, amchur powder, and salt. Mix well, garnish with coriander leaves, and let it cool completely.
  2. Cut the Pastry: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Roll out the thawed puff pastry sheet on a floured surface and cut it into equal-sized rectangular blocks.
  3. Assemble the Puffs: Place a generous spoonful of the cooled aloo masala on one half of each rectangle. Top the potato filling with a layer of grated cheese, leaving the edges clear.
  4. Seal the Pastry: Fold the empty half of the pastry over the filling to form a rectangular packet. Press the outer edges down firmly with your fingers, or crimp them with a fork to lock the seal. Score light diagonal slits across the top surface for venting.
  5. Bake to Golden: Arrange the puffs on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Lightly brush the top of each puff with milk or melted butter for a glossy finish. Bake for 20 to 25 minutes until the pastry completely puffs up into flaky layers and turns an even golden brown. Serve hot.
