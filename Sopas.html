SOPAS

This Hearty Beef Macaroni Soup is a comforting, all-in-one meal perfect for cold days. Tender elbow macaroni is simmered alongside savory ground beef, sweet corn, diced carrots, celery, and juicy tomatoes in a rich, herb-infused tomato broth. Thick, wholesome, and packed with garden vegetables, this classic home-style soup delivers a nostalgic and deeply satisfying dining experience.

INGREDIENTS

  • Meat: ½ lb lean ground beef (or minced beef)
  • Pasta: 1 cup elbow macaroni noodles
  • Vegetables: ½ cup sweet corn kernels, ½ cup finely diced carrots, ½ cup chopped celery, ½ cup diced onions, and 1 cup chopped tomatoes
  • Broth Base: 4 cups beef broth (or water with beef bouillon cubes) and 1 cup tomato sauce
  • Aromatics: 3 cloves garlic, finely minced
  • Herbs & Spices: 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning, ½ tsp paprika, salt, and black pepper to taste
  • Fat: 1 tbsp olive oil for sautéing

DIRECTIONS

  1. Brown the Beef: Heat olive oil in a large deep pot over medium-high heat. Add the ground beef and cook for 5 to 6 minutes, breaking it apart with a spoon, until it is completely browned. Drain off any excess fat.
  2. Sauté the Vegetables: Toss the minced garlic, diced onions, carrots, and celery directly into the pot with the beef. Sauté for about 3 to 4 minutes until the onions turn soft and translucent.
  3. Simmer the Liquid Base: Pour in the beef broth, tomato sauce, diced tomatoes, sweet corn, Italian seasoning, and paprika. Bring the soup mixture to a rolling boil, then lower the heat to medium-low, cover, and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Cook the Macaroni: Stir the dry elbow macaroni into the simmering broth. Keep the pot uncovered and cook for another 8 to 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the pasta is perfectly tender.
  5. Season and Serve: Taste the broth and season with extra salt and freshly cracked black pepper as needed. Ladle the hot soup into deep bowls and serve immediately alongside warm crusty bread.
