LEMON RASPBERRY MUFFIN

These bright and summery muffins are light, moist, and infused with freshly grated lemon zest. Baked in the traditional way, they feature juicy, bursting fresh raspberries inside and are topped with a rich, velvety swirl of sweet lemon buttercream frosting.

INGREDIENTS

    For the Muffins:

    • 150g soft unsalted butter
    • 150g light brown caster sugar
    • 3 medium eggs (whisked)
    • 150g self-raising flour (sifted)
    • 2 tablespoons lemon zest (finely grated)
    • 150g fresh raspberries

    For the Lemon Buttercream:

    • 150g unsalted butter (room temperature)
    • 350g icing sugar
    • 1-2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice (or lemon extract)
    • Fresh raspberries and extra lemon zest (for garnish)

DIRECTIONS

  1. Preheat & Prep: Preheat your oven to 150°C (300°F) Fan. Line a 12-hole muffin/cupcake tin with paper cases.
  2. Cream Butter & Sugar: Cube the 150g of soft butter into a mixing bowl. Add the caster sugar and cream together using an electric mixer until light, pale, and fluffy.
  3. Add Eggs: Keep the mixer running on high speed and gradually stream in the whisked eggs a little at a time until completely incorporated.
  4. Combine Flour & Zest: Gently fold the sifted self-raising flour and finely grated lemon zest into the wet mixture until a smooth cake batter forms.
  5. Add Fruit: Gently fold the fresh raspberries into the batter, taking care not to crush them too much.
  6. Bake: Divide the muffin mix evenly among the paper liners. Bake in the oven for 20 minutes, or until cooked through and springy to the touch. Transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
  7. Make Frosting: Beat the room-temperature butter with an electric mixer until ultra-smooth. Gradually mix in the icing sugar followed by the fresh lemon juice until a light, pipeable consistency is achieved.
  8. Assemble & Decorate: Transfer the lemon buttercream to a piping bag fitted with a star nozzle. Pipe tall swirls onto each cooled muffin. Garnish with a fresh raspberry and a sprinkle of lemon zest.
