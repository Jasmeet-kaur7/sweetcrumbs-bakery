MANGO MAJESTY CAKE

Our signature Mother's Day Mango Majesty Cake is a sun-kissed celebration of seasonal flavor—featuring layers of ultra-light, moist vanilla sponge infused with fresh Alphonso mango purée. Enrobed in a silky white chocolate buttercream frosting, the cake is elegantly accented with a vibrant, glossy mango glaze drip, then crowned with juicy mango cubes and white chocolate curls. Bright, tropical, and unapologetically luxurious—it is a regal dessert experience crafted to celebrate Mom.

INGREDIENTS

    For the Mango Vanilla Sponge Layers:

    • 250g All-Purpose Flour (Sifted)
    • 200g Caster Sugar
    • 1.5 teaspoons Baking Powder
    • 0.5 teaspoon Baking Soda
    • 0.5 teaspoon Salt
    • 3 Large Eggs (Room temperature)
    • 100g Unsalted Butter (Melted and cooled)
    • 120ml Fresh Mango Purée (Alphonso or Kesar variety preferred)
    • 60ml Whole Milk
    • 1 teaspoon Pure Vanilla Extract

    For the Buttercream Frosting & Mango Drip:

    • 200g Unsalted Butter (Softened to room temperature)
    • 400g Powdered Icing Sugar (Sifted)
    • 100g White Chocolate (Melted and cooled for the frosting base)
    • 100ml Fresh Mango Purée + 1 tablespoon Cornstarch (Simmered into a thick glaze for the drip)
    • 1 cup Fresh Mango (Diced into neat cubes for the topping)
    • White Chocolate Shavings or Curls (For garnish)

DIRECTIONS

  1. Bake the Sponges: Preheat your oven to 350°F (177°C). Grease and line two 6-inch round cake pans. Whisk the dry ingredients together in a large bowl. In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs, melted butter, mango purée, milk, and vanilla extract. Combine the wet and dry ingredients, stir until smooth, and divide between pans. Bake for 28-32 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean. Cool completely.
  2. Whip the White Chocolate Buttercream: Beat the softened butter for 3 minutes until light and creamy. Gradually incorporate the icing sugar and vanilla. Pour in the melted and cooled white chocolate, beating on high speed for another 2 minutes until completely smooth and velvety.
  3. Prepare the Mango Drip: Simmer the remaining 100ml of mango purée and cornstarch in a small saucepan over low heat for 3 minutes until thick and glossy. Let it cool completely to room temperature before attempting to create the drip effect.
  4. Assemble the Tiers: Slice your cooled sponges horizontally to create 4 uniform layers. Place the base layer on a cake stand, spread a layer of white chocolate buttercream, and add a few small pieces of fresh chopped mango. Repeat the process with the remaining layers.
  5. Crumb Coat and Frost: Cover the outside of the cake with a smooth, sharp layer of buttercream. Use a bench scraper to achieve pristine, sharp vertical sides. Chill the cake in the refrigerator for 30 minutes to set the frosting before adding the drip.
  6. Apply the Majesty Drip & Garnish: Transfer the cooled mango glaze to a squeeze bottle or use a spoon to carefully create elegant drips down the sides of the chilled cake. Frost the top center, then crown it heavily with diced fresh mango cubes and white chocolate curls. Finish with an elegant gold "best MOM ever" topper.
