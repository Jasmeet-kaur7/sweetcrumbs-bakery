SPICY PANEER ROLL

The Spicy Paneer Roll features a fusion twist, packing rich Indian flavors inside a soft, toasted hot dog bun. The heart of this dish is a fiery, tangy masala hash made with succulent cubes of paneer (cottage cheese) tossed in a robust tomato-onion gravy. Garnishments of fresh coriander, spicy green mint chutney, and sweet tomato ketchup make this an irresistible accompaniment to a warm glass of chai.

INGREDIENTS

  • Buns: 2 fresh hot dog buns or sub rolls
  • Paneer: 1 cup paneer, cut into small bite-sized cubes
  • Aromatics: 1 finely chopped medium onion & 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • Base: ½ cup finely chopped tomatoes or pureed tomato
  • Spices: ½ tsp red chilli powder, ½ tsp garam masala, ¼ tsp turmeric, and salt
  • Herbs: 2 tbsp fresh coriander leaves, finely chopped
  • Sauces: 1 tbsp tomato ketchup & 1 tbsp green mint-coriander chutney
  • Butter/Oil: 1.5 tbsp for cooking and lightly toasting the buns

DIRECTIONS

  1. Sauté Aromatics: Heat oil or butter in a pan over medium flame. Add the chopped onions and fry for 3 minutes until translucent, then stir in the ginger-garlic paste until fragrant.
  2. Build the Gravy Base: Add the chopped tomatoes along with the red chilli powder, garam masala, turmeric, and salt. Cook down for 4 to 5 minutes until the tomatoes soften and separate from the oil.
  3. Toss the Paneer: Lower the flame and gently fold in the paneer cubes. Stir well to ensure the spicy masala coats the cheese evenly. Simmer for 2 minutes, then stir in fresh coriander.
  4. Toast the Buns: Slice the hot dog buns down the top center without cutting all the way through. Lightly toast the buns with butter on a flat skillet until warm and slightly golden.
  5. Assemble and Serve: Spread a thin layer of green chutney and ketchup inside the bun slit. Generously stuff the hollow with the hot, spicy paneer mixture. Top with extra coriander and serve hot with tea.
