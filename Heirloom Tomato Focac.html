HEIRLOOM TOMATO FOCACCIA

Our Artisanal Heirloom Tomato Focaccia celebrates traditional high-hydration Italian baking with a vibrant garden twist. Generously dimpled by hand and steeped in cold-pressed extra virgin olive oil, our signature dough slow-proofs to create a web of light, airy pockets and a tender interior. Studded with sliced, juicy heirloom tomatoes that caramelize into sweet, concentrated gems during baking, it is finished with fresh picked rosemary sprigs and flakes of hand-harvested sea salt.

INGREDIENTS

  • High-Hydration Dough: 3 ½ cups premium unbleached bread flour, 1 packet instant yeast, 1 ½ cups warm spring water
  • Baking Oil: ½ cup cold-pressed extra virgin olive oil (divided for coating and drizzling)
  • Garden Toppings: 1 cup multi-colored heirloom cherry tomatoes (halved or sliced thick)
  • Aromatics & Finish: 3 sprigs of fresh organic rosemary, 1 tbsp Maldon flaky sea salt, 1 tsp organic honey

DIRECTIONS

  1. Mix and Hydrate: Stir the instant yeast and honey into lukewarm water until dissolved. In a large bowl, whisk bread flour with a teaspoon of fine salt, then combine with the yeast liquid and two tablespoons of olive oil using a spatula to form a wet, sticky dough.
  2. Slow Rise Cycle: Drizzle a tablespoon of olive oil across the surface of the wet dough. Cover the bowl securely and allow it to rise undisturbed at room temperature for 4 hours to let large internal air bubbles develop.
  3. Pan Proofing Phase: Coat a standard baking sheet or rectangular pan with two tablespoons of olive oil. Gently slide the airy dough into the pan without deflating the structure. Flip once to lubricate both sides, stretch gently toward the pan edges, and proof uncovered for 2 hours until incredibly puffy.
  4. Dimple and Garnish: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Pour the remaining olive oil evenly over the expanded dough. Coat your fingers in oil and press them firmly straight down into the dough to form deep dimples. Press the sliced heirloom tomatoes and fresh rosemary leaves deep into the pockets, finishing with a generous sprinkle of flaky sea salt.
  5. Bake to a Crisp: Bake for 25 to 30 minutes until the top is heavily blistered golden-brown and the bottom crust is crisp and dark. Transfer immediately to a wire rack to cool slightly before cutting into artisanal thick squares to serve.
