MAVA CAKE

Our Signature Mava Cake is a premium take on a traditional time-honored classic. Slowly baked with caramelized milk solids (khoya) and pure clarified butter, this golden loaf boasts an extraordinarily soft, melt-in-your-mouth texture. Fragrant undertones of fresh green cardamom warm each bite, creating a beautifully balanced, rich heritage treat designed exclusively for the afternoon tea table.

INGREDIENTS

  • Mava Base: 1 cup fresh unsweetened milk solids (khoya / mava), crumbled smoothly
  • Dry Mix: 1 ½ cups premium cake flour, 1 tsp baking powder, and a pinch of salt
  • Rich Fats: ½ cup pure unsalted butter (softened) and ¼ cup heavy cream milk
  • Sweetener & Binding: 1 cup fine castor sugar and 3 large farm eggs
  • Aromatics: 1 tsp freshly ground green cardamom seeds and ¼ tsp pure vanilla extract
  • Garnish Option: A fine dusting of slivered almonds and pistachios (optional)

DIRECTIONS

  1. Prep the Oven & Baking Pan: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line your standard loaf pan evenly with heavy parchment paper, leaving generous flaps along the outer borders for easy removal.
  2. Prepare the Mava: Lightly heat the crumbled mava in a dry skillet over low heat for 2-3 minutes until slightly softened and fragrant. Set aside to cool down completely before mixing into the main batter.
  3. Cream Butter & Sugar: In a large bowl, cream the softened unsalted butter and castor sugar together using a hand mixer until the mixture turns pale, light, and wonderfully fluffy.
  4. Add Eggs & Aromatics: Incorporate the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Gently blend in the cooled mava solids, milk cream, ground cardamom powder, and vanilla extract until uniform.
  5. Fold & Bake: Sift the cake flour and baking powder directly over your wet ingredients. Use a wide spatula to fold everything together smoothly until just combined. Pour into the prepared loaf pan and bake for 40 to 45 minutes until the crust turns a rich golden-brown color and a toothpick comes out clean. Let cool completely before serving.
