CHOCOLATE COOKIES

Our signature double chocolate cookies are a masterclass in texture—crisped to perfection at the edges with a dense, decadently chewy heart that melts at the first touch. Rich, comforting, and unapologetically indulgent—they’re not just a sweet treat; they’re an exquisite moment of pure, nostalgic bliss in every single bite.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup Unsalted Butter (Cold, cubed into small pieces)
  • 0.75 cup Light Brown Sugar (Packed firmly for rich moisture)
  • 0.5 cup Granulated White Sugar
  • 2 Large Eggs (Cold, straight from the refrigerator)
  • 2 cups All-Purpose Flour (Sifted)
  • 0.75 cup Dutch-Process Cocoa Powder (High-quality for deep color and flavor)
  • 1 teaspoon Cornstarch (Gives the centers a thick, ultra-tender crumb)
  • 0.75 teaspoon Baking Soda
  • 0.5 teaspoon Fine Sea Salt
  • 2 cups Semi-Sweet Chocolate Chips or Chunks (Plus extra for topping)

    DIRECTIONS

    1. Cream the Base: In a stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment, beat the cold, cubed butter with the brown sugar and white sugar on medium-high speed for 3 to 4 minutes. The mixture should become thoroughly combined and lightened, but remain cool.
    2. Emulsify Cold Eggs: Add the eggs one at a time, beating thoroughly on medium speed after each addition until completely smooth and incorporated.
    3. Sift Dry Cocoa Mix: In a separate bowl, whisk together the sifted all-purpose flour, Dutch-process cocoa powder, cornstarch, baking soda, and fine sea salt until uniform.
    4. Merge the Dough: Turn the mixer speed down to low. Gradually add the dry flour blend to the wet cream, mixing until just combined. Gently fold in the semi-sweet chocolate chips with a heavy spatula to avoid overworking the gluten.
    5. Shape into Mountains: Divide the heavy dough into 6 to 8 massive, rustic portions (roughly 4 to 6 ounces each). Do not roll them into smooth balls; keep the texture textured and craggy to encourage distinct crisp crevices during the bake.
    6. The Shock Freeze: Place the raw portioned dough chunks onto a tray lined with parchment paper and freeze for at least 1 hour. Baking from a completely frozen state stops the massive cookies from flattening out into puddles.
    7. High-Heat Bakery Bake: Preheat your oven to 415°F (212°C). Arrange the frozen cookies 3 inches apart on a cold baking sheet. Bake for 10 to 12 minutes. The outside crust should look fully set and cracked, while the interiors stay completely molten and soft.
    8. Rest to Solidify: Let the delicate cookies settle directly on the hot baking tray for 10 minutes to finish setting their structure. Move them carefully onto a wire cooling rack to rest completely before diving in.
