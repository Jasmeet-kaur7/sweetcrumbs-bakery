FUDGE BROWNIE À LA MODE

Our Signature Fudge Brownie à la Mode is a luxurious study in sweet culinary contrasts. Crafted from premium melted dark chocolate couverture and loaded with chopped roasted walnuts, this ultra-dense, intensely fudgy square balances a rich crinkle-top layer with a velvety core. Crowned with an artisanal scoop of cold vanilla bean ice cream, a lattice of rich chocolate syrup, and a fresh mint sprig, it offers an uncompromised fine-dining finale.

INGREDIENTS

  • Rich Fudgy Base: 1 cup premium 70% dark chocolate chunks (melted), ½ cup unsalted butter
  • Dry Matrix: ¾ cup unbleached all-purpose flour, ¼ cup Dutch-process cocoa powder, ¼ tsp salt
  • Textured Crunch: ½ cup whole English walnuts (slow-roasted and roughly chopped)
  • Sweeteners & Binding: 1 cup fine castor sugar, 2 large farm eggs, 1 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • Ice Cream Topping: 1 premium scoop of organic vanilla bean ice cream
  • Drizzle & Garnish: 2 tbsp dark chocolate syrup, 1 fresh picked mint leaf sprig

DIRECTIONS

  1. Prep Your Oven & Tin: Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Line an 8x8-inch square metal baking tin cleanly with non-stick parchment paper, leaving an overhang on the sides for clean lifting.
  2. Melt the Chocolate Base: Combine the dark chocolate chunks and unsalted butter in a heatproof bowl. Place over a saucepan of gently simmering water (double boiler) and stir continuously until completely melted, smooth, and glossy. Remove and cool to room temp.
  3. Whisk the Egg Mixture: In a medium bowl, vigorously beat the eggs, castor sugar, and vanilla extract together with a whisk for 2-3 minutes until pale, thick, and slightly aerated to ensure a perfect crinkle-top crust.
  4. Combine and Fold: Slowly pour the cooled, melted chocolate butter mixture into the beaten eggs. Sift the all-purpose flour, cocoa powder, and sea salt directly over the liquid base, folding gently with a rubber spatula before tossing in the roasted walnut pieces.
  5. Bake and Assemble: Spread the thick batter smoothly into your lined tin. Bake for 22 to 25 minutes until the edges are set but the center remains slightly soft. Cool completely before cutting into large squares. Serve a warm brownie square on a plate, top immediately with a scoop of cold vanilla bean ice cream, zig-zag the chocolate syrup, and finish with a fresh mint leaf.
