PENNE PASTA

This Penne Pomodoro is a timeless Italian masterpiece celebrating simplicity and fresh ingredients. Ridged penne rigate pasta tubes are lightly coated in a sweet, slow-simmered sauce made from crushed vine-ripened tomatoes, extra virgin olive oil, and aromatic garlic. Elegantly finished with a crowning sprig of fragrant fresh green basil leaves, this dish delivers a pure, clean, and comforting flavor profile.

INGREDIENTS

  • Pasta: 250g penne rigate (ridged penne noodles)
  • Tomatoes: 1.5 cups crushed Italian plum tomatoes (or high-quality passata)
  • Aromatics: 3 cloves garlic, peeled and gently smashed
  • Herbs: 1 large bunch fresh basil leaves (separated for cooking and garnish)
  • Fat: 3 tbsp premium extra virgin olive oil
  • Seasoning: Salt and freshly cracked black pepper to taste

DIRECTIONS

  1. Boil the Penne: Cook the penne pasta in a deep pot of rolling, heavily salted boiling water until it reaches a firm al dente texture. Drain cleanly, reserving about ¼ cup of starchy pasta water.
  2. Infuse the Olive Oil: Heat extra virgin olive oil in a wide skillet over medium-low heat. Add the smashed garlic cloves and a few sprigs of fresh basil. Sauté for 2 minutes to completely infuse the oil, ensuring the garlic does not brown.
  3. Simmer the Pomodoro Sauce: Pour in the crushed tomatoes and season lightly with salt and black pepper. Stir gently and bring to a light simmer over medium heat. Cook uncovered for 10 minutes until the sauce slightly thickens and reduces. Discard the garlic cloves.
  4. Combine and Glaze: Toss the cooked penne directly into the simmering sauce. Pour in 2 tablespoons of the reserved pasta water. Turn the heat up to medium-high and toss continuously for 1 minute until the glossy tomato sauce tightly coats the ridges of the pasta.
  5. Garnish and Serve: Turn off the flame. Plate the hot pasta in a wide ceramic bowl and top immediately with fresh whole basil leaves and a light extra drizzle of olive oil.
