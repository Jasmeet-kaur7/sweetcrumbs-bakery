BUTTER -RICH CROISSANT AU BEURRE

Master the ultimate art of French baking with this traditional recipe. These croissants are deeply golden, incredibly flaky, and packed with rich butter flavor. When pulled apart, they reveal a beautifully airy, wide-open honeycomb structure.

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g All-Purpose Flour or T55 French Flour
  • 60g Sugar
  • 10g Fine Sea Salt
  • 10g Instant Dry Yeast
  • 140g Whole Milk (Cold)
  • 140g Water (Cold)
  • 40g Unsalted Butter (Softened, for the base dough)
  • 250g Premium European-Style Butter (Chilled, minimum 82% fat content for rolling)
  • 1 Large Egg + 1 tbsp Milk (For the egg wash)

DIRECTIONS

  1. Make the Base Dough (Détrempe): Whisk flour, sugar, salt, and yeast together in a stand mixer. Add the cold milk, water, and 40g of softened butter. Knead with a dough hook on medium-low for 6 minutes until a smooth, sturdy dough forms. Shape into a flat rectangle, wrap tightly, and chill in the refrigerator overnight.
  2. Prepare the Butter Block (Beurrage): Place your 250g of cold European butter between two sheets of parchment paper. Use a rolling pin to firmly pound and roll it into a flat, even 7x7-inch square. Keep it in the fridge so it stays firm but pliable.
  3. Lock in the Butter: Roll your chilled dough into a 10x10-inch square. Place the butter block diagonally in the center of the dough. Fold the corners of the dough over the butter to completely enclose it, pinching the seams tightly to seal it like an envelope.
  4. Perform the Folds (Lamination): Roll the dough out lengthwise into a long rectangle. Fold the bottom third up, then fold the top third down over it (like a letter). Turn the dough 90 degrees, roll it out long again, and repeat the fold. Wrap in plastic and rest in the freezer for 20 minutes. Repeat this process one final time for a total of 3 simple folds.
  5. Shape the Croissants: Roll the final rested dough out into a long rectangle roughly 4mm thick. Slice into tall triangles (approx. 4 inches wide at the base and 10 inches long). Make a tiny 1/2-inch slit in the center of the base, then roll the triangle up tightly from the base to the tip.
  6. Proof Until Puffed: Arrange the croissants on a parchment-lined baking sheet, leaving plenty of space between them. Let them rise at room temperature (around 68°F–72°F / 20°C–22°C) for 2 to 2.5 hours until they double in size, show visible flaky layers, and wiggle gently when shaken.
  7. Bake Until Golden: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Gently brush the tops of the proofed croissants with the egg wash. Bake for 15-18 minutes until they expand into a brilliant, deep-golden brown color. Cool completely on a wire rack.
